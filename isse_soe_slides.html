States of Emergency: Causes and Consequences

ISSE Project

Research Questions

  1. What drives states of emergency declarations?
    • Understanding the predictors and conditions that lead governments to declare states of emergency
  2. What are the consequences of states of emergency?
    • Examining impacts on human rights violations, conflict, and other key outcomes

Two-Model Framework

Model 1: SOE as Outcome (DV)

  • Dependent Variable: State of emergency declaration
  • Key Question: What political, economic, and social factors predict SOE declarations?

Model 2: SOE as Predictor (IV)

  • Independent Variable: State of emergency declaration
  • Dependent Variables:
    • Human rights violations
    • Conflict intensity/incidence
    • Other outcomes of interest

Model 1: Predicting SOE Declarations

Potential Predictors:

  • Political instability
  • Economic crises
  • Security threats
  • Institutional factors
  • International pressure
  • Electoral cycles
  • Previous SOE history

Model 2: SOE Impact on Outcomes

Key Outcomes to Examine:

  • Human rights violations
    • Arbitrary detention
    • Freedom of expression restrictions
    • Assembly/association rights
  • Conflict-related outcomes
    • Armed conflict incidence
    • Protest suppression
    • State violence
  • Democratic backsliding indicators

Methodological Considerations

  • Temporal dynamics: Duration effects of SOEs
  • Endogeneity concerns: SOEs both cause and are caused by instability
  • Measurement: Coding SOE declarations across countries/time
  • Heterogeneity: Different types of emergencies (health, security, economic)
  • Control variables: Country-level factors, regional trends, temporal effects

Data Requirements

  • SOE declarations: Cross-national data on timing, type, and duration
  • Human rights data: CIRI, V-Dem, Amnesty International, etc.
  • Conflict data: UCDP, ACLED, PRIO
  • Covariates: Political institutions, economic indicators, demographics
  • Time period: TBD based on data availability

Expected Contributions

  • Systematic evidence on what triggers SOE rhetoric and declarations
  • Causal evidence on SOE consequences for rights and conflict
  • Understanding mechanisms linking emergencies to outcomes
  • Policy implications for monitoring and accountability
